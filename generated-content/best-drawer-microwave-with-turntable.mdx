## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Drawer Microwave With Turntable

Investing in a drawer microwave is a significant upgrade for your kitchen. Unlike countertop models, they are a permanent fixture, so it’s crucial to make the right choice. Here’s a detailed guide to the key factors you should consider.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you even look at models, assess your kitchen and your habits. Measure the cabinet space where you plan to install the microwave. Most drawers are designed for standard 24-inch or 30-inch wide cabinets. Ensure you have adequate depth and height, as well as access to a proper electrical outlet. Consider how you use a microwave. Are you just reheating coffee and leftovers, or do you cook entire meals? If you’re a power user, look for models with more features, higher wattage, and convection capabilities. If your needs are simple, a basic, reliable model will suffice.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Drawer microwaves are a premium appliance, and their price reflects that. Expect to spend significantly more than you would on a countertop or over-the-range model. Prices typically range from $1,200 to over $2,500. The lower end will get you a solid, reliable model with basic functions. As you move up in price, you'll find features like smart-home connectivity, convection cooking, touchless opening mechanisms, and more refined designs. Set a realistic budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
