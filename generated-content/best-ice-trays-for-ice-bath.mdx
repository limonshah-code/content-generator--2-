## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Ice Trays For Ice Bath

### Find Your Need
Before purchasing, consider the size of your cold plunge tub. A small portable tub might only need 10 pounds of ice to reach the desired temperature, while a large permanent tank or bathtub may require 30 to 50 pounds. If you are focused on facial therapy, a specialized bowl is better than bulky block molds.

### Budget
Realistic price ranges for high-quality silicone ice molds typically fall between 20 and 60 dollars. Budget options usually provide 2 to 4 molds, while premium or bulk sets offer 6 or more molds with added features like lids. Investing more upfront often saves money in the long run by providing a higher volume of ice per freeze cycle.

### Key Features
Look for thickened silicone walls and reinforced rims. These features prevent the mold from bowing out and creating misshapen ice blocks that are hard to stack. Another key feature is the inclusion of lids, which allows you to stack the trays even when the water is still liquid, maximizing your freezer efficiency.

### Quality & Durability
Avoid cheap, thin plastic trays that can crack when the water expands during freezing. Food-grade silicone is the gold standard because it remains flexible at sub-zero temperatures, making it easy to remove the ice. Look for molds that specify a wide temperature range, as this indicates higher-quality material.

### Brand Reputation
