## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Floor Lamp For Reading

### 1. Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you hit the buy button, think about where the lamp will live. Are you placing it behind a deep armchair, or do you need it to hover over a craft table? If you read in bed, a lamp with a timer and a remote is vital. If you are an artist, look for full-spectrum or high-CRI (Color Rendering Index) lighting to ensure colors look accurate. Compatibility with your current furniture height is also key; adjustable height lamps like the O’Bright model are great for this.

### 2. Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Floor lamps for reading typically fall into three price brackets. Entry-level options ($30-$50) like the ROTTOGOON offer basic functionality and classic styles. ### 3. Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Lumens measure brightness; for reading, look for at least 800 to 1000 lumens. Color temperature is also critical—3000K is warm and cozy, while 5000K-6000K mimics daylight and is better for focus. Look for stepless dimming if you want total control, or preset modes if you prefer simplicity. A flexible gooseneck or swing arm is almost mandatory for a reading lamp so you can avoid glare on your pages.

### 4. Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Metal construction is always superior to plastic for longevity and heat dissipation. A weighted base is a non-negotiable safety feature, especially if you have children or pets who might bump into the lamp. Check the rated lifespan of the LED; most quality lamps offer 20,000 to 50,000 hours, which equates to many years of daily use.

Brands like Verilux and Brightech have built their reputations on lighting science and customer support. Consider the footprint of the base. If you have limited space, look for a slim, heavy base that can slide under a couch or chair. The height of the lamp also matters; it should be tall enough to cast light over your shoulder but not so tall that the bulb is visible and blinding.
